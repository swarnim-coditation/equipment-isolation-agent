# OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 — Control of Hazardous Energy (Lockout/Tagout)

Authoritative reference for the agent. Source: U.S. Occupational Safety and Health
Administration, 29 CFR 1910.147 (https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.147),
including non-mandatory Appendix A. This is the regulatory basis for the order of
operations in any isolation / lockout-tagout (LOTO) procedure.

---

## 1. Scope and purpose

1910.147(a)(1)(i): This standard covers the servicing and maintenance of machines
and equipment in which the **unexpected energization or start up** of the machines
or equipment, or **release of stored energy**, could cause injury to employees. It
establishes **minimum performance requirements** for the control of such hazardous
energy.

Energy source (1910.147(b)): any source of electrical, mechanical, hydraulic,
pneumatic, chemical, thermal, or other energy. Our domain primarily deals with
**process** energy (pressurized fluids, gases, steam).

---

## 2. Key definitions (1910.147(b))

- **Energy isolating device**: a mechanical device that physically prevents the
  transmission or release of energy — e.g. a manually operated electrical circuit
  breaker, a disconnect switch, a **line valve**, a **block**, blank flanges, and
  bolted slip blinds. Push buttons and selector switches are NOT energy isolating
  devices.
- **Lockout**: placement of a lockout device on an energy isolating device so the
  device and equipment cannot be operated until the lockout device is removed.
- **Lockout device**: a device using a positive means (a key or combination lock)
  to hold an energy isolating device in a safe position. **Blank flanges and
  bolted slip blinds are lockout devices.**
- **Energized**: connected to an energy source OR containing residual/stored energy.

---

## 3. Mandatory sequence of control — 1910.147(d)

> 1910.147(d): The procedures for the application of energy control "shall cover
> the following elements and actions and **shall be done in the following sequence**."

This sequence is **fixed by regulation**. The phases MUST be performed in this
order; reordering them is an OSHA violation and a safety hazard.

### Phase 1 — Preparation for shutdown (d)(1)
> Before turning off a machine or equipment, the authorized employee shall have
> knowledge of the **type and magnitude of the energy**, the hazards of the
> energy to be controlled, and the **method or means to control the energy**.

### Phase 2 — Machine or equipment shutdown (d)(2)
> The machine or equipment shall be turned off or shut down using the procedures
> established for the machine or equipment. An **orderly shutdown** must be
> utilized to avoid any additional or increased hazard(s).

### Phase 3 — Machine or equipment isolation (d)(3)
> All energy isolating devices needed to control the energy shall be physically
> located and operated to isolate the machine or equipment from the energy
> source(s). This is where valves are closed, breakers opened, blinds installed.

### Phase 4 — Lockout or tagout device application (d)(4)
> Lockout/tagout devices shall be affixed to each energy isolating device.
> Lockout devices shall hold the energy isolating devices in a "safe" or "off"
> position.

### Phase 5 — Stored or residual energy (d)(5)
> Following application of lockout devices, all potentially hazardous **stored or
> residual energy shall be relieved, disconnected, restrained, and otherwise
> rendered safe** (e.g. bleeding down pressure, venting, draining). If there is a
> possibility of **reaccumulation** of stored energy to a hazardous level,
> verification of isolation shall be **continued** until servicing is completed.

### Phase 6 — Verification of isolation (d)(6)
> Prior to starting work, the authorized employee shall verify that isolation and
> deenergization have been accomplished (e.g. operate the normal control, check a
> pressure gauge reads zero, open a test/bleed point and confirm no flow/pressure).

## 4. Release from lockout/tagout — 1910.147(e)

Before locks/tags are removed and energy is restored:
1. Inspect the work area — nonessential items removed, components intact (e)(1).
2. Ensure all employees are safely positioned/removed (e)(2).
3. Each lockout/tagout device removed by the employee who applied it (e)(3).

Restoration of energy generally follows the **reverse** direction of isolation.

---

## 5. Typical minimal lockout procedure (Appendix A — non-mandatory template)

OSHA's Appendix A provides a field-usable procedure template. Sequence of lockout:

1. **Notify** all affected employees that servicing/maintenance is required and the
   equipment must be shut down and locked out.
2. The authorized employee identifies the **type and magnitude of the energy**,
   understands the hazards, and knows the methods to control the energy.
3. If operating, **shut down** by the normal stopping procedure (depress stop
   button, open switch, close valve, etc.).
4. **De-activate the energy isolating device(s)** so the equipment is isolated from
   the energy source(s).
5. **Lock out** the energy isolating device(s) with assigned individual lock(s).
6. **Stored or residual energy** (capacitors, springs, elevated members, rotating
   flywheels, hydraulic systems, air/gas/steam/water pressure) must be
   **dissipated or restrained** by grounding, repositioning, blocking, bleeding down.
7. **Verify isolation** — first check no personnel are exposed, then verify by
   operating the normal control(s) or testing to make certain the equipment will
   not operate. Caution: return operating control(s) to neutral/"off" after verifying.
8. The machine or equipment is now locked out.

Restoring equipment to service:
1. Check machine/equipment and area — nonessential items removed, components intact.
2. Check work area — all employees safely positioned/removed.
3. Verify controls are in neutral.
4. Remove lockout devices and reenergize.
5. Notify affected employees that servicing is complete and equipment is ready for use.

## 6. Equipment-isolation mapping notes

For process equipment isolation (valves/blinds/flanges on a P&ID):
- **Phase 3 isolation devices** = the selected isolation points (valves to close &
  lock, blinds/spades to install, flanges to break).
- **Phase 5 stored-energy relief** = bleed, vent, and drain points (often the
  verification_candidate set in our evidence). If none were found on the P&ID, this
  is a mandatory **field-verification gap** — energy may be trapped.
- **Phase 6 verification** = pressure gauges, pressure indicators, or test points
  near the isolated section used to confirm zero energy.
- **Within-phase ordering** (e.g. which of several valves to close first) is
  engineering judgment informed by: isolate upstream/inlet before downstream to
  avoid backflow; isolate the highest-energy source first; follow the process flow.
  The graph traversal direction (source_component → candidate) can inform this.
